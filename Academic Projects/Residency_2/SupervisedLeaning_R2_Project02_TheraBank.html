Question 1 - Understanding Dataset¶

The columns ID, Age, Experience denote the identification number, completed years in age and the total work/professional experience of the customer¶

The Income colums gives the annual income of the customers in thousands of dollars (if the entry is 2 it implies the annual income is 2000 USD)¶

Zipcode column gives us the area where the cutomer resides.¶

Family column denotes the number of people in the customer's family¶

CCavg is the amount spent on the credit card per month on an average in thousands of dollars (if the entry is 1.5 it implies the amount spent is 1500 USD)¶

Education in coded in such a way that 1--> Customer is an Undergraduate student, 2 --> He/She is a Graduate, 3 --> Customer is in higher studies or a working professinal¶

Mortgage is the value of the customer's house mortgage in thousands of dollars (if the entry is 200 it implies the value is 200000 USD)¶

Personal Loan is a binary column showing if the customer had accepted loan in the last campaign¶

Securities account is a binary column showing if the customer has securities account with the bank. Securities are tradables like equity, debt, derivatives etc which are held at the bank usually traded with.¶

CD account is a binary column showing if the customer has a certificate of deposit (similar to FD longer period of unmoved money in the account for higher interest)¶

Online is a binary column showing if the customer uses internet banking facilities (0 --> no 1 --> yes)¶

CreditCard is a binary column showing if the customer uses credit card issued by Universal Bank (0 --> no 1 --> yes)¶

Question 7 - Observation from the models¶

In this particular problem, what we desire is to correctly identify the customers who have a higher probability of taking a loan from this bank so that we can target market for them. So the priority goes for $less$ $false$ $negatives$ and $high$ $true$ $positives$ so that the bank doesn't miss any potential loan borrowers. From the confusion matrix it is clear that the Logistic Regression gives better results for this particular problem.¶

Here Logistic Regression works best because the target variable is a binary class and Logistic Regression is used in classification when a lot of numerical independent variable are present to determine the target variable¶
